## Who This Is For

Sales operations managers, SDR leads, and founders at 20-150 person companies who are running email-based lead capture campaigns and need forms to sync directly into their CRM without breaking email deliverability. You care about inbox placement, reliable data capture, and minimal friction between inbound inquiry and CRM entry. You're not building a survey tool or public feedback form. You're converting email recipients into pipeline.

## What You Actually Get

**Mailmodo** is an email-native forms platform. Forms render directly inside Gmail, Outlook, and other email clients without requiring users to click through to a landing page. You get conditional logic, file uploads, signature capture, and payments. It connects to HubSpot, Pipedrive, Salesforce, Monday.com, and integrates with Zapier. There's a free tier with basic forms and up to 50 responses per month. Paid plans include unlimited responses, advanced integrations, and SMTP relay for sending forms embedded in your own email campaigns. Mailmodo also offers email deliverability features like domain verification and DKIM setup because the whole product exists to keep forms in the inbox.

**Typeform** is a visual, web-based form builder. Forms are responsive, beautiful, and render on mobile and desktop equally well. You get conditional logic, branching, file uploads, and integrations with 500+ apps via Zapier and native connectors. Typeform creates unique URLs for each form and tracks link performance. It has a free tier with limited responses and basic branding. Paid plans ($25-$99/month) unlock custom domains, logic jumps, payment collection, and priority support. Typeform is built for surveys, customer feedback, and sign-up funnels. It does not embed directly in email.

<Callout type="info" title="Email deliverability matters in B2B lead capture">
  If you're embedding forms inside cold email or marketing email campaigns, the form's delivery mechanism directly impacts inbox placement. Forms that live in the email client (Mailmodo) don't trigger external link clicks that spam filters flag. Forms that require click-through to landing pages (Typeform) create additional friction and potential deliverability red flags in high-volume outreach.
</Callout>

## Where It Shines

**Mailmodo shines when you're embedding forms in email campaigns.** The core feature is forms that render directly in Gmail, Outlook, and Apple Mail without requiring a click-out. Recipients see the form, fill it, and hit submit without leaving their inbox. This cuts form abandonment by 40-60% versus link-based forms. In B2B outreach, where you're sending 50-200 emails per week and trying to qualify leads fast, this matters. You send an email with an embedded qualification form, recipient answers four questions right in Gmail, and their response lands in your CRM. Mailmodo's form submission speeds are fast (sub-second rendering for most clients).

**Mailmodo also integrates email sending directly into the product.** You can send forms as part of campaigns from within Mailmodo itself, or use their API to programmatically send email + form combinations. If you're using tools like Instantly or Lemlist for cold email delivery, Mailmodo's SMTP relay lets you send forms from your own domain without creating separate tracking domains. This keeps your sender reputation intact because you're not adding extra links or redirects that trigger spam filters.

**Typeform shines when forms are destination pages, not inbox embeds.** Landing pages, product sign-ups, feedback surveys, and customer intake forms all work well on Typeform. The visual builder is faster to set up than Mailmodo's, and the form design is beautiful without customization. If you need forms on a website or accessible via QR code, Typeform is the practical choice. The branching logic is also more mature. Ask question A, and if the answer is "yes," skip to question C. Typeform handles this elegantly. Mailmodo supports branching too, but Typeform's feels more refined for complex survey flows.

## Where It Disappoints

**Mailmodo's form builder is less visually refined than Typeform.** If you care about branding and want forms that look premium, Typeform wins. Mailmodo forms are functional and clean, but they look like forms. There's no Typeform-style question-per-screen design or motion effects. If your form is a public landing page or customer-facing intake, Typeform looks better.

**Mailmodo's email sending infrastructure requires manual domain setup.** If you want to send forms from your own domain (which you should for deliverability), you need to configure SPF, DKIM, and DMARC. Mailmodo provides the records, but the setup is on you. If you've never touched email infrastructure, this is friction. Typeform avoids this entirely because it doesn't send email.

**Typeform has weaker CRM integrations.** The native HubSpot connector exists, but it's basic. If you use Pipedrive or Salesforce, you're routing through Zapier, which adds latency and limits custom field mapping. Mailmodo's CRM integrations map fields bidirectionally and sync faster. For cold email workflows where timing matters (a prospect responds to your form and you want to follow up within 30 minutes), Typeform's integration lag becomes noticeable.

## Pricing: What You'll Actually Pay

**Mailmodo:**
- Free: Up to 50 form submissions/month, basic integrations, no custom domain
- Standard: $19/month (1 user, 500 submissions/month, up to 3 forms, CRM integrations)
- Pro: $49/month (3 users, 5,000 submissions/month, unlimited forms, SMTP relay, advanced integrations)
- Business: $99/month (5 users, 50,000 submissions/month, priority support, advanced analytics, white-label options)

Annual billing saves 20%. Each additional user costs $15-25/month depending on plan. SMTP relay for email sending is included on all paid tiers, which is crucial if you're sending campaign email + forms.

**Typeform:**
- Free: 10 responses/month, limited integrations, Typeform branding required
- Basic: $25/month (100 responses/month, remove Typeform branding, custom domain)
- Plus: $50/month (1,000 responses/month, payment collection, advanced logic, priority support)
- Business: $99/month (10,000 responses/month, team collaboration, advanced permissions, white-label)

Annual billing saves 20-25%. All paid plans include 500+ app integrations via Zapier, but custom field mapping on CRM integrations requires manual setup.

<Notice>
  If you're sending cold email with embedded forms, Mailmodo's SMTP relay on the Pro plan ($49/month) is cheaper than running separate email infrastructure or paying per-send fees on tools like Instantly or Lemlist. Typeform doesn't solve the email sending problem at all, so you'd still need a separate tool.
</Notice>

## Verdict

Use **Mailmodo** if you're an operations manager or sales leader running email-based lead qualification campaigns where response speed and inbox placement matter. You're sending 50+ emails per week with embedded forms, and you need data in your CRM within minutes. The Pro plan ($49/month) includes SMTP relay, so you're not doubling up on email infrastructure costs. This is the right fit for SDR teams, sales consulting firms, and founders doing outbound prospecting.

Use **Typeform** if your forms live on public landing pages, website footers, or customer intake flows where you're not worried about email deliverability or link click friction. You need beautiful, branching-logic-heavy surveys or feedback forms. If your sales team is using traditional marketing pages and newsletter sign-ups (not cold email), Typeform's design and integration ecosystem win.

If you're running [Apollo vs Hunter: B2B Email Finder Tool Comparison for Sales Teams](https://telotonet.com/articles/apollo-vs-hunter-b2b-email-finder-tool-comparison-for-sales-teams/) and need to qualify prospects via email, Mailmodo is the natural follow-on. If you're building signup flows or customer feedback loops independent of email, Typeform is the faster, more polished choice. The decision hinges on whether your forms live in the inbox or on the web.

## Who This Is For

You're an operations manager, founder, or sales lead running a 5-50 person team. You're drowning in Slack threads, status update emails, and recurring meetings that could have been a 2-minute video. You want something faster than scheduling a call, easier than writing out context in a paragraph, and lightweight enough that your team won't resist using it. Loom and Wistia both solve this, but they solve it differently.

## What You Actually Get

Loom lets you record your screen, camera, or both. You hit record, talk through a process or decision, and share a link. No editing required. No uploading to a platform. It works as a Chrome extension, desktop app, or in-browser recorder. You get instant link sharing, basic playback controls, and the ability to add calls-to-action (CTAs) to drive clicks. Loom's free tier includes 5 videos per month with basic sharing. Paid plans unlock unlimited videos, transcripts, and custom domains.

Wistia is a video hosting and analytics platform. You upload or record video, customize the player with your branding, track viewer behavior (who watched, when they dropped off, which parts they rewound), and embed or share with access controls. Wistia's strength is the data layer. You see heatmaps showing which moments viewers engaged with most, conversion tracking tied to CTAs, and individual viewer reports. It feels more like a video infrastructure tool than a quick-share solution.

<Callout type="info" title="The core difference">
  Loom is built for speed and internal sharing. Wistia is built for control, branding, and measurement. Choose Loom if you're replacing meetings. Choose Wistia if you're also tracking whether those videos move people to action.
</Callout>

## Where It Shines

**Loom excels at replacing synchronous meetings.** Hit record, explain the problem, save 45 minutes of calendar wrestling. No onboarding, no training. A new team member can record and share a 3-minute video explaining their workflow in less time than it takes to write an email about it. The clip is searchable (full transcripts are included), embeddable in docs, and shareable via link. For teams running 5-10 daily standups across time zones, Loom cuts meeting bloat dramatically. The free tier is genuinely usable for small teams, so you can test it without budget approval.

**Wistia excels when you need proof of engagement.** You send a product walkthrough video to a prospect and see that they watched 87% of it and rewound the pricing section three times. That's data that tells you they care and what matters most. For sales teams sending demo videos, for product teams sharing feature announcements, for customer success teams providing training, Wistia's heatmaps and engagement reports create accountability. You know who consumed what. That matters when you're trying to figure out if a video explanation actually landed or if people are ignoring it anyway.

**Wistia also wins on polish.** Custom branding, branded players, domain customization, password protection, and email capture gates transform a simple video into a branded asset. If your video is part of your product or sales experience, not just an internal note, Wistia looks professional. Loom's video player is minimal and functional, not branded.

## Where It Disappoints

**Loom's limitations show up at scale.** The transcript search is basic. Organizing dozens of videos across projects is clunky. There's no folder structure or tagging system that feels natural. If your team produces 50+ videos per month, you'll struggle to find the walkthrough you recorded three months ago. Commenting is minimal (you can add reactions to timestamps, but no threaded discussion). Video quality can vary depending on your recording setup, and Loom doesn't offer transcoding or quality optimization. For larger teams or longer-term video libraries, Loom starts to feel like a recording tool, not a knowledge management system.

**Wistia's main pain point is friction and price.** Recording directly in Wistia is slower than Loom. You often end up recording in Loom, then uploading to Wistia, which defeats the purpose. The interface is heavier. It's overkill if all you need is "record and share with the team." The analytics are powerful, but they add cognitive load. Not every internal video needs heatmaps. And the pricing scales quickly; Wistia's entry point is higher, and you're paying for features many small teams don't need.

## Pricing: What You'll Actually Pay

**Loom:**
- Free: 5 videos per month, 5-minute clip limit, basic sharing. Genuinely functional for testing.
- Starter ($13/month billed annually or $18/month monthly): Unlimited videos, unlimited video length, up to 10 custom domains, transcript search, chat reactions.
- Business ($25/month billed annually or $30/month monthly): Everything in Starter, plus custom branding, advanced sharing controls, priority support.

The free tier means you can replace three or four weekly meetings without paying anything. Most small teams land on Starter because transcript search and custom domains become useful once you're shipping multiple videos per week.

**Wistia:**
Free tier is truly limited: one project, three videos, no analytics. It's a demo, not a working tier.
- Starter ($99/month): 1 project, 10 GB storage, analytics, heatmaps, email capture, basic branding. Year commitment saves about 20%.
- Standard ($349/month): 3 projects, 100 GB storage, all analytics features, password protection, API access.
- Advanced ($999/month): 10 projects, 1 TB storage, custom domain, advanced integrations, priority support.

The real cost is Starter at $99/month or $1,188/year. If you commit annually, it's around $79/month. For a 15-person team, that's under $6/person/month. For a 5-person team, it's $20/person/month, which starts to feel heavy if you're only using it for internal status videos.

<Notice>
  Wistia's pricing doesn't include per-video or per-viewer costs, which is good. But the minimum commitment of $99/month makes it a harder sell than Loom's freemium model for teams under 10 people.
</Notice>

## Verdict

Use **Loom** if you're a small team (under 20 people) where async communication is mostly internal. You're replacing standups, onboarding calls, and long explanatory emails with quick video clips. You don't need viewer analytics. The free or $13/month plan handles your entire workflow. Setup takes minutes, and your team will actually use it because there's no friction.

Use **Wistia** if you're sending videos to clients or customers, or if you need to know whether people actually watched and engaged with what you sent. You're willing to spend $99/month for professional branding, heatmaps, and confidence that your video communication landed. Sales teams, customer success teams, and product teams often land here because the data justifies the cost. For purely internal use at a startup, Loom is a smarter spend.

The honest middle ground: Many teams use both. Loom for speed (team updates, quick demos, feedback), Wistia for strategy (client videos, product walkthroughs, educational content). If you're just starting to replace meetings with video, begin with Loom free, prove the value, then add Wistia when you have external videos worth tracking. Don't lock yourself into $99/month upfront for a habit that hasn't formed yet.

## The Problem This Stack Solves

You're managing outbound email infrastructure for 50+ client domains. Each client has different lists, compliance requirements, and deliverability baselines. Unverified email addresses tank your bounce rates. Domain reputation gets tanked by stale addresses or complaint-prone contacts. You're juggling verification tools, bounce monitoring, suppression lists, and compliance audit trails across clients. Without a coherent hygiene stack, you're burning sender reputation and losing client trust.

This stack prioritizes pre-send validation, real-time bounce monitoring, and per-domain suppression management at a price point that doesn't require justifying spend to every single client.

<Callout type="info" title="Why agencies fail at list hygiene">
Most agencies try to use a single tool for everything and end up with mixed data, compliance issues, and no per-client audit trail. The stack below separates concerns: validation happens before send, monitoring happens after, and suppression is managed per domain to keep clients isolated.
</Callout>

## Tool 1: ZeroBounce

ZeroBounce sits at the front of your funnel. Before any email hits a domain, it validates every address against multiple databases and syntax checks. You're not just checking if an email exists—you're checking if it's catch-all, role-based, or dormant.

For agencies managing 50+ client domains, ZeroBounce's batch processing is non-negotiable. Upload a CSV, tag it by client domain, and get back a scored list with confidence ratings. The API integration means you can build this into your pre-send workflow without manual uploads.

Key config: Set up separate uploads per client or per campaign. ZeroBounce tracks your credit usage across all uploads, so you'll need to monitor monthly burn rates. At scale, you're looking at roughly 2–5 credits per 1,000 addresses validated. Keep a spreadsheet of client list sizes so you don't blow budget mid-month.

The free tier gives you 100 credits to test. Upgrade to the $99 plan for 100K monthly credits. If you're validating lists for 50+ clients at 500–5,000 addresses each, you'll use roughly 50–80K credits monthly, staying comfortably within the paid tier.

## Tool 2: Validity (formerly Return Path)

Validity is the reputation scorecard. While ZeroBounce cleans your list before send, Validity monitors what happens after. It tracks your sender reputation across domains, flagges authentication issues (DKIM, SPF, DMARC failures), and surfaces deliverability blockers in real time.

For agencies, this is where you catch when a client's domain is getting spam-trapped or blacklisted. Validity pulls data from all major ISPs and blocklists, so you're not flying blind after campaign send. It costs more than alternatives, but the compliance and audit trail value justifies it when you're managing multiple brands.

Set it up by adding each client domain to your Validity account. The platform will monitor authentication records and flag misconfigurations. Most agencies create a weekly report showing domain score, any new blocklist entries, and authentication gaps. Share that report with clients as a value-add.

One constraint: Validity doesn't directly manage suppression lists. It flags problems; you fix them manually or through your email sending platform. This is intentional—it keeps Validity lean and focused on monitoring.

## Tool 3: RocketReach

RocketReach is your fallback and repair tool. After ZeroBounce validates a list, some addresses still fail or bounce in production. RocketReach's reverse email lookup lets you find alternative contact methods or updated addresses for that failed segment.

Use it sparingly and tactically. Don't rely on it as a primary validator—it's slower and more expensive per lookup. But if a client has a mission-critical list with a 15% bounce rate and you need to salvage revenue, RocketReach can rebuild a portion of that list or find alternatives.

The $40 plan gives you monthly lookups. Keep it as a reserve tool, not your primary flow. Most agencies budget 10–15% of their list budget for repair work.

## Tool 4: Google Workspace + Gmail API

This is the hidden layer most agencies ignore. Google Workspace (Gmail for Business) integrates with your sending infrastructure and tracks bounces natively. If you're managing sender domains in Gmail or forwarding through Google's infrastructure, you already have bounce data—you just need to extract it.

Use the Gmail API to pull bounce logs per domain, categorize them (hard bounce, soft bounce, unsubscribe), and automatically feed them into suppression lists. This is critical for compliance: every domain should have its own bounce suppression, not a global blacklist.

Set up a Gmail account per client domain (or use service accounts), then use Zapier to pull bounce events and update a shared suppression sheet. This keeps suppression data isolated per client, which matters for audits and client separation.

The cost is $14/user in Google Workspace. If you're managing 50+ client domains, you won't need 50+ separate Gmail accounts—typically 2–3 shared service accounts across all domains is sufficient.

<Notice>
If you're using a sending platform like Lemlist, Instantly, or Smartlead, you already have bounce data in their native dashboards. The Gmail API layer is only necessary if you're also forwarding through Gmail or managing suppression outside your sending tool.
</Notice>

## Tool 5: Zapier

Zapier is the glue. It automates the entire flow: ZeroBounce validation triggers a Zapier action, results get tagged by client, and hard-bounce addresses automatically flow into suppression lists in your sending platform.

Set up these core workflows:

1. **ZeroBounce → Spreadsheet**: Every validated list gets appended to a master validation log by client domain.
2. **Gmail API → Spreadsheet**: Daily bounce logs from each domain feed into a shared suppression tracker.
3. **Spreadsheet → Sending Platform**: Your ESP (Lemlist, Instantly, etc.) reads the suppression list and excludes hard bounces on the next send.

The $30 plan gives you 750 tasks/month. At scale, you're probably hitting 200–400 tasks monthly (validation runs, bounce checks, suppression updates), so you'll stay well within limits.

## How the Tools Connect

The workflow flows like this:

1. **Pre-Send (Day 1)**: Client submits a list of 5,000 addresses for domain A. Upload to ZeroBounce, tag it "Client A - Campaign X." ZeroBounce validates and returns a clean list minus catch-alls and dormants (typically 80–90% valid).

2. **Zapier Automation**: ZeroBounce results trigger a Zapier action that appends the validated addresses to a "Client A - Clean Lists" spreadsheet. Invalid addresses go to a "Client A - Suppression" sheet.

3. **Send**: Send the clean list through your ESP (Lemlist, Instantly, etc.) from Client A's authenticated domain.

4. **Post-Send (Days 2–7)**: Gmail API (or your ESP's native bounce tracking) logs hard and soft bounces from Client A's domain. Zapier pulls those bounces daily and appends them to the Client A suppression sheet.

5. **Monitoring**: Validity monitors Client A's domain reputation, flags authentication gaps, and alerts you if the domain hits any blocklists.

6. **Weekly Review**: Pull a report showing per-domain bounce rates, suppression growth, and any reputation flags. Share with clients.

7. **Ongoing**: Next campaign on Client A's domain reads the suppression sheet before sending, excluding all previous hard bounces.

This flow scales to 50+ domains because each client has isolated data and a consistent workflow.

## What to Swap If Your Budget Is Different

**Tighter budget ($150/month):**
- Drop Validity. Use ZeroBounce alone for validation and rely on your ESP's native bounce tracking for monitoring. Trade: No independent domain reputation monitoring, but you'll catch major blocklist issues through bounces.
- Swap RocketReach for a free alternative like Hunter.io's basic plan.
- Keep Google Workspace (you probably already have it) and Zapier.
- **New total: ~$170/month**

**Loose budget ($500+/month):**
- Add Postmark or SendGrid for dedicated transactional monitoring on top of ZeroBounce.
- Add Everest (formerly HubSpot's reputation tool) for advanced domain reputation modeling.
- Keep everything above and layer in per-client whitelisting services (Validity Premium tier).
- **New total: ~$500–600/month**

**Mid-market alternative (80+ clients):**
- Consider Deliverability.com or Return Path (Validity) enterprise licensing. These offer volume discounts and dedicated account management once you hit a certain threshold (typically 20+ domains or $2K+/month in volume).

## Bottom Line

This stack is purpose-built for agencies managing 50+ client domains where data isolation and per-domain compliance matter. ZeroBounce handles validation at scale, Validity monitors reputation independently, and Zapier glues it all into a workflow that's repeatable and auditable per client.

You're not paying for fancy personalization features or advanced segmentation. You're paying for clean lists, visible domain health, and an audit trail that survives a client review.

**This stack is perfect for:**
- Agencies running cold outbound or bulk email campaigns across multiple brands
- Teams managing 50+ sender domains with compliance/audit requirements
- Operations leaders who need per-client deliverability reporting
- Anyone who's burned sender reputation and needs to rebuild methodically

**Look elsewhere if:**
- You're managing fewer than 10 domains (overkill and overpriced)
- You only send warm, in-house email (your ESP's native tooling is enough)
- You need warmup as part of your stack (add a tool like Warm, Smartlead, or Instantly separately)

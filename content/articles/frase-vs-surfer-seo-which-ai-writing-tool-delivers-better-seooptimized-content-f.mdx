## Who This Is For

You're a marketing manager or operations lead at a company with 5-50 people. Your blog is responsible for lead generation, but your in-house content production is slow. You're managing 1-3 writers who either struggle to balance keyword optimization with readability, or you're writing most posts yourself and burning out. You need a tool that speeds up first drafts without requiring extensive prompts and that actually improves your search rankings, not just adds keywords.

## What You Actually Get

Frase and Surfer SEO both combine AI writing assistance with real-time SEO analysis. They show you keyword gaps, competitor content structures, and reading-level recommendations as you write. Neither tool writes full posts in isolation. Both require you to feed them a topic, keyword, or competitor URL, then they guide the writing process.

Frase gives you an AI co-writer that understands your target keyword and competitor landscape. You input a topic and the tool analyzes the top 10 ranking pages, then generates outlines, full drafts, or just section headers. It includes a content brief feature that summarizes what competitors are covering, topic gaps, and recommended word count. The AI writing part feels integrated into the research phase. Frase also includes a Q&A finder that surfaces real user questions from forums and search console data, though the data quality depends on your niche.

Surfer SEO is positioned as a keyword research and content optimization platform with AI writing bolted on. You run a content audit on your keyword, see a visual comparison against top-ranking competitors (word count, headings, entities, backlinks), then use the AI to fill in sections. Surfer's strength is its visual content editor where you see real-time SEO scoring as you write. Every sentence you add gets instant feedback on keyword density, readability, and semantic relevance. The platform also includes a rank tracker and content calendar, which Frase doesn't offer natively.

Neither tool writes full, publication-ready articles without heavy human input. Expect 40-60% of the work to come from the AI, with the remaining work being fact-checking, voice editing, and strategic rewrites. Both tools have hard limits on monthly content pieces and word count that you need to understand before buying.

<Callout type="info" title="Critical limitation both tools share">
Neither Frase nor Surfer SEO does well with niche or emerging topics where competitor data is thin. If your blog covers a specialized vertical with fewer than 5-10 ranking competitors, their recommendations become generic. Test both with your actual content topics before committing.
</Callout>

## Where It Shines

**Frase excels at research-to-outline speed.** If you're starting from scratch on a new topic, Frase's content brief is genuinely faster than manual research. It pulls together competitor analysis, suggests internal linking opportunities, and surfaces common questions in 60 seconds. The outline it generates is structured around actual search intent gaps, not just keyword density. This saves your writers 30-45 minutes per piece. The AI writing suggestions stay contextual to those gaps rather than forcing keyword stuffing.

**Surfer SEO dominates visual content optimization and team workflows.** If you have multiple writers, Surfer's shared projects and collaborative editing make version control and SEO handoff cleaner. More importantly, Surfer's real-time content editor shows keyword density, reading level, and semantic relevance as you type, which means writers get immediate feedback instead of guessing at SEO optimization. The rank tracker integration is also useful if you need to correlate content updates with traffic changes in one interface.

**Frase works better for long-form research content and cornerstone pages.** Its AI can handle complex, multi-section topics better because it understands topic clusters. If your strategy includes pillar pages with supporting cluster content, Frase's ability to suggest internal linking and topic relationships is more mature.

## Where It Disappoints

Frase's writing quality is inconsistent for brand voice. The AI tends toward generic marketing language. You'll rewrite almost every AI-generated paragraph to match your actual tone. This undermines the time savings for small teams with established voice standards.

Surfer's rank tracker is barebones compared to tools like SEMrush or Ahrefs. If you're choosing it partly for tracking, you'll likely need a separate rank tracking tool anyway, which adds cost and tool bloat.

Both tools struggle with updating existing content. They're built for new-from-scratch writing, not refreshing old posts that need minor SEO updates. If 30% of your monthly effort is refreshing published content, neither tool saves meaningful time.

Frase's Q&A feature requires you to have search console data connected and doesn't work well for blogs with minimal query volume. For new sites or niche blogs, the recommendations turn vague.

Surfer's monthly content quotas are deceptively low. Even the mid-tier plan (Surfer Pro) only includes 10 new content pieces per month and 100,000 AI words. For a team publishing 2-3 posts per week, you'll hit the word limit before the piece limit, forcing an upgrade.

Neither tool flags factual accuracy or outdated information. Both will confidently write false claims if competitor content contains them. You need a human review step for any claims about data, statistics, or product features.

## Pricing: What You'll Actually Pay

**Frase pricing:**
- Basic: $14.99/month (single user, 10 posts/month, limited AI words)
- Pro: $44.99/month (2 users, 30 posts/month, more AI features, research briefs)
- Business: $114.99/month (5 users, unlimited posts, full team collaboration)

The catch: the free trial is 7 days, and the Basic plan's 10 post limit makes it near-useless for any active blog. Most small teams need Pro, which costs $540/year. You also hit AI word limits before post limits on lower tiers, which isn't transparent upfront. Frase doesn't list exact word counts, just "limited" vs. "more" on the plan page. In practice, the Pro plan gives you roughly 50,000-75,000 AI-generated words monthly, which is enough for 10-15 posts if you're using the AI for sections only, not full drafts.

**Surfer SEO pricing:**
- Starter: $19/month (3 projects, 5 content pieces/month)
- Pro: $99/month (unlimited projects, 10 content pieces/month, 100,000 AI words)
- Business: $299/month (unlimited everything, 5 team seats)

Surfer's Business plan is steep, and overage charges for content pieces run $20 per piece, which adds up fast. The AI word limit on Pro is 100,000/month, but that's less than it sounds. A 2,000-word blog post uses 2,000 words from your quota, so you're capped at 50 full posts per month before hitting the word limit. For a team publishing 2-3 posts weekly, you need the Business plan or you'll overage regularly. The Business plan ($299/month or $3,588/year) is where Surfer becomes viable for mid-size content teams.

If you're publishing 8-12 posts per month with 1-2 writers, Frase Pro ($45/month) costs less and handles your volume. If you're publishing 12+ posts monthly with 3+ writers, Surfer Business is cheaper on a per-post basis than Frase Business, but you're paying $299/month upfront.

<Notice>
Both tools often run promotions for annual billing. Frase gives 40% off annual plans (Frase Pro drops to $27/month paid yearly). Surfer does 33% off annual. If you're undecided, sign up for the annual plan discount but commit to testing for 2-3 weeks first to confirm the tool fits your actual workflow.
</Notice>

## Verdict

Use Frase if you're a small content team (1-3 writers) publishing 8-15 posts monthly and you need faster research and outline generation. The content brief and Q&A features genuinely save research time, and the lower price point ($45/month for Pro) scales better for modest publishing volume. You'll still rewrite the AI-generated copy for voice, but you're buying time back on the research half of the process.

Use Surfer SEO if you have 3+ writers managing parallel projects and you need them to ship SEO-optimized first drafts without an SEO reviewer's involvement. Surfer's visual editor and real-time scoring means writers can self-optimize. The rank tracker is useful if you tie content performance to your publishing schedule. Budget for the Business plan ($299/month) if you're shipping 12+ posts monthly. The mid-tier Pro plan will force overages and frustration at that volume.

If cost is the primary constraint and you're comfortable with less SEO-specific guidance, consider combining ChatGPT Plus ($20/month) with a separate tool like SEMrush's content assistant or free Ubersuggest for keyword research. You lose the integrated workflow, but you save $25-80/month. For teams with tight budgets and writing discipline, that approach often works better than a half-measure AI writing tool.
